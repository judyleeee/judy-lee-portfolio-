## Problem

Samsung.com's Manuals & Downloads page served millions of users globally · a post-purchase touchpoint where brand loyalty either holds or fractures. **At scale, it was fracturing.**

  columns={3}

Most users were abandoning self-service and escalating to support. Every failed lookup converted into a support contact · a downstream cost compounding into support overhead, churn risk, and eroded brand trust at the exact moment that determines long-term loyalty.

## Hypothesis

The surface story was "navigation is broken." The deeper pattern was different · every pain point traced back to one moment: users couldn't confirm they were looking at *their* product.

<Callout>
  Users didn't need more options or smarter search · they needed <strong>confirmation</strong> at the decision moment. Reduce cognitive load there · not before it · and completion should rise.
</Callout>

**Alternatives considered and ruled out**

- A pure search-only fix · ignores users who don't know the model name
- A deeper taxonomy · adds clicks, worsens the 40s metric

The path forward had to serve both audiences in parallel.

## Approach

One redesign · three named UI patterns working in parallel. Each pattern targeted a specific failure mode uncovered in research.

## Key Learning

<Callout>
  Research revealed identity confusion, not search intent.
</Callout>

Reframing the problem as a moment of confirmation · rather than a navigation failure · was what changed the design direction.

Small craft decisions · visual hierarchy, instant verification cues · compound into measurable business outcomes when they land at the right moment. The real leverage wasn't restructuring the navigation. It was inserting a confirmation signal at the precise point where users were abandoning.

> Verification-first design outperforms navigation-first design when the user's real problem is identity confirmation · not discovery.

## What I'd Test Next

**Honest limitation.** Validation happened post-launch against behavioral data · not through pre-launch usability testing with real users. Some design choices weren't fully de-risked before ship.

Next iteration would:

- Run moderated usability sessions earlier to validate the taxonomy before implementation
- Layer in proactive model-detection (via serial number or purchase history) to collapse selection time toward zero for returning users
